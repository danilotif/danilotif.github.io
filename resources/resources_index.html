Productivity & Time Management

  • Parkinson's Law - Work expands to fill available time
  • Hofstadter's Law - Tasks always take longer than expected, even accounting for this law
  • Student Syndrome - Starting work only at the last possible moment
  • Pareto Principle (80/20 Rule) - 80% of results come from 20% of effort
  • Pomodoro Technique Effect - Breaking work into intervals improves focus
  • Zeigarnik Effect - Unfinished tasks are remembered better than completed ones
  • Planning Fallacy - Tendency to underestimate time needed for tasks
  • Two-Minute Rule - If it takes less than 2 minutes, do it now
  • Eisenhower Matrix Principle - Urgent vs important task prioritization
  • Timeboxing Law - Fixed time periods force efficiency

Organizational Behavior

  • Peter Principle - People rise to their level of incompetence
  • Pournelle's Iron Law of Bureaucracy - Organizations prioritize self-preservation over mission
  • Conway's Law - Organizations design systems mirroring their communication structure
  • Brooks's Law - Adding people to late projects makes them later
  • Dunning-Kruger Effect - Incompetent people overestimate their abilities
  • Imposter Syndrome - Competent people underestimate their abilities
  • Hierarchical Incompetence - Authority and expertise don't always align
  • Shirky Principle - Institutions try to preserve problems they exist to solve
  • Putt's Law - Technology is dominated by incompetents
  • Dilbert Principle - Incompetent employees are promoted to management

Cognitive Biases & Decision Making

  • Confirmation Bias - Seeking information that confirms existing beliefs
  • Anchoring Bias - Over-relying on first information received
  • Availability Heuristic - Judging probability by ease of recall
  • Sunk Cost Fallacy - Continuing investment based on previously invested resources
  • Choice Overload - Too many options reduce decision quality
  • Analysis Paralysis - Over-analyzing prevents decision-making
  • Hindsight Bias - Past events seem more predictable than they were
  • Survivorship Bias - Focusing on successful examples while ignoring failures
  • Fundamental Attribution Error - Attributing others' actions to character vs circumstances
  • Halo Effect - One positive trait influences overall perception

UX & Design Psychology

  • Hick's Law - Decision time increases with number of choices
  • Fitts's Law - Time to reach a target depends on distance and size
  • Cognitive Load - Mental effort required to process information
  • Serial Position Effect - First and last items remembered better
  • Peak-End Rule - Experiences judged by peak and ending moments
  • Goal Gradient Effect - Effort increases as goals get closer
  • Default Bias - People stick with pre-selected options
  • Framing Effect - How information is presented affects decisions
  • Decoy Effect - Inferior options make others more attractive
  • Scarcity Principle - Limited availability increases perceived value
  • Loss Aversion - Losses feel worse than equivalent gains feel good
  • Endowment Effect - We overvalue what we already own
  • IKEA Effect - We value things we build ourselves more
  • Commitment & Consistency - We honor past commitments and decisions
  • Negativity Bias - Negative experiences have greater impact than positive
  • Decision Fatigue - Decision quality deteriorates after many decisions

Economic & Market Behaviors

  • Goodhart's Law - When a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be good
  • Campbell's Law - Metrics lose value when used for decision-making
  • Jevons Paradox - Efficiency improvements increase total consumption
  • Veblen Effect - Higher prices increase demand for luxury goods
  • Network Effect - Product value increases with more users
  • Winner-Take-All Effect - Small advantages compound dramatically
  • Gresham's Law - Bad money drives out good money
  • Say's Law - Supply creates its own demand
  • Parkinson's Second Law - Expenditures rise to meet income
  • Price Anchoring - First price seen influences all subsequent price judgments

Communication & Information

  • Shannon's Limit - Maximum rate of error-free information transmission
  • Signal-to-Noise Ratio - Useful information vs irrelevant data
  • Chinese Whispers Effect - Information degrades through transmission
  • Wadsworth Constant - First 30% of content can usually be skipped
  • Betteridge's Law - Headlines ending in questions can be answered with 'no'
  • Cunningham's Law - Best way to get right answer is to post wrong answer online
  • Poe's Law - Extreme views are indistinguishable from parodies without clear intent
  • Godwin's Law - Online discussions eventually involve Hitler comparisons
  • Streisand Effect - Attempts to suppress information increase its spread
  • Metcalfe's Law - Network value proportional to square of connected users

Software Development & Technology

  • Moore's Law - Computing power doubles every two years
  • Wirth's Law - Software gets slower faster than hardware gets faster
  • Hofstadter-MÃ¶ller Law - Software complexity grows faster than hardware capability
  • Linus's Law - Given enough eyes, all bugs are shallow
  • Murphy's Law - Anything that can go wrong will go wrong
  • Finagle's Law - Anything that can go wrong will, at the worst possible time
  • Clarke's Third Law - Sufficiently advanced technology is indistinguishable from magic
  • Postel's Law - Be conservative in what you send, liberal in what you accept
  • Zawinski's Law - Every program attempts to expand until it can read mail

Management & Leadership

  • Dunbar's Number - Stable social relationships limited to ~150 people
  • Ringelmann Effect - Individual effort decreases as group size increases
  • Social Loafing - People exert less effort in groups
  • Groupthink - Desire for harmony results in poor decisions
  • Paradox of Choice - Too many options decrease satisfaction
  • Delegation Paradox - Those who need to delegate most are least likely to
  • Micromanagement Trap - Over-control reduces team performance
  • Peter's Corollary - In hierarchies, work is accomplished by those not yet promoted
  • Putt's Corollary - Every technical hierarchy develops incompetence
  • Hanlon's Razor - Never attribute to malice what can be explained by stupidity

Social Psychology

  • Bystander Effect - People less likely to help when others are present
  • Milgram Effect - People obey authority even when it conflicts with conscience
  • Stanford Prison Experiment Effect - Roles powerfully influence behavior
  • Hawthorne Effect - People modify behavior when they know they're observed
  • Placebo Effect - Belief in treatment causes real improvement
  • Bandwagon Effect - People adopt beliefs because others have
  • Authority Bias - People attribute greater accuracy to authority figures
  • In-Group Bias - Favoring members of one's own group
  • Social Proof - People copy actions of others in uncertain situations
  • Reciprocity Principle - People feel obligated to return favors

Systems Theory & Complexity

  • Butterfly Effect - Small changes can have large consequences
  • Parkinson's Law of Triviality - Time spent on issues inversely related to importance
  • Occam's Razor - Simplest explanation is usually correct
  • Chesterton's Fence - Don't remove barriers until you understand their purpose
  • Unintended Consequences - Actions have effects that weren't foreseen
  • Cobra Effect - Solutions make problems worse
  • Lucas Critique - Economic relationships change when policies change
  • Feedback Loops - Outputs influence inputs in complex systems
